Research

In our team, we strive to master the newest technologies and to be aware of latest research. Also, we feel ourselfs responsible to do our bit in sharing knowledge to our students. In return, students do relevant studies which are not always directly in scope with a current project in our organisation. In this section we provide an overview of our academic research.

Monitoring on Sulphur Dioxide Emissions

Gerrit-Jan de Bruin

In this thesis different technologies were compared which allow to remotely monitor sulphur dioxide emissions from the shipping industry. A well-reported estimate for the fraction of ships sailing on compliant fuel is given.

Predicting Fuel Sulphur Violations

Patrick Schulz

We try to predict whether a given merchant ships will contain more fuel sulphur content than allowed during Port State Control.

Imputation Methods Outperform Missing-Indicator for Data Missing Completely at Random

António Pereira Barata et al.

This work provides tools and insights about dealing with partial data. This is often the case in inspection data, as not every aspect of an object is known or can be too expensive to obtain.

Finding Anomalies in Waste Transportation Data with Supervised Category Models

António Pereira Barata, Gerrit Jan de Bruin, et al.

In this work we apply several tools from the data science domain to find anomolies in the Waste Shipment Regulation. We ultimately aim to discriminate between regular and non-regular behavior.

Understanding Behavioral Patterns in Truck Co-driving Networks

Gerrit Jan de Bruin, et al.

This paper examines the co-driving behavior of truck drivers using network analysis. The resulting improved understanding of co-driving behavior has important implications for society and the environment, as trucks coordinating their driving behavior together help reduce traffic congestion and optimize fuel usage.

Understanding Dynamics of Truck Co-Driving Networks

Gerrit Jan de Bruin, et al.

To understand the dynamics of the truck co-driving network, we use a link prediction approach employing a machine learning classifier. Findings suggest that the dynamics of a truck co-driving network has clear social network effects.
